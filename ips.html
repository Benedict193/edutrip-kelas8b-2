Jiufen Old Street, located in Ruifang District, New Taipei City, Taiwan, is a historic area that traces its origins back to the Qing Dynasty when it was a small village housing nine families, hence the name "Jiufen," meaning "nine portions". The discovery of gold in the late 19th century transformed Jiufen into a bustling mining town, reaching its peak during Japanese colonization, which left a lasting architectural influence. After World War II and the decline of gold mining, Jiufen fell into obscurity until the 1989 film A City of Sadness brought it back into the spotlight. Today, Jiufen Old Street is a popular tourist destination, celebrated for its narrow alleyways, traditional teahouses, and vibrant cultural heritage.

Keelung Night Market, also known as Miaokou Night Market, is a crowded place located near Dianji Temple in Keelung City, Taiwan. It features vibrant streets lined with food stalls offering a variety of Taiwanese foods, such as mini steaks, butter crabs, and boba. Visitors can enjoy the lively atmosphere, due to all the game stalls, shopping for unique items, and eat freshly prepared street food while navigating through the crowded yet organized alleys. The market is a popular destination for both locals and tourists looking for a night market to enjoy.
